Manulex_Infra and Manulex_Morpho are two lexical databases describing the written vocabulary encountered by French elementary school children according to the orthographic, grapho-phonological and morphological characteristics of the written words (publication references below). The databases were created to assist researchers and educators who need to study and analyze the distributional characteristics of orthographic, grapho-phonological and morphological patterns in the French language in order to design literacy instruction programs.The second version (dec.2021) of the databases can be downloaded from the website. Version 2 integrates major modifications.

The analyses described in Manulex_Infra (ver. 2) and Manulex_Morpho (ver. 2) are based on the Manulex word corpus, a lexical corpus corresponding to nearly 49,000 words found in 54 French elementary school books. The development of Manulex_Infra and Manulex_Morpho was motivated by the observation that analyses of child-directed written material were limited to statistical estimates of word occurrence. In spite of the undeniable relevance of the word-frequency norms for studying literacy acquisition, word frequency is obviously not the sole factor influencing reading and writing performance, so more specialized databases are needed. In particular, it is well known that the statistical characteristics of a language, such as the consistency of the mappings between orthography and phonology, influence literacy acquisition.The aim of Manulex_infra was thus to provide quantitative estimates for several infra-lexical variables (syllable, grapheme-to-phoneme mappings, bigrams), and lexical variables (lexical neighborhood, homophony and homography). Unlike Manulex_infra, the morphological structure of the written words is also considered in Manulex_morpho when computing the consistency of orthography-phonology mappings (inflectional morphology, silent word-ending pronounced in inflected/derived forms).

The current version of these two databases is v.2 for Manulex-Infra and v.2 for Manulex-Morpho. Several major changes and corrections have been made to previous versions. (12/2021; 01/2022)
