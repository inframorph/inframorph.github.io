Description

The Eqol_infra database was developed from the EQOL corpus (Stanké et al., 2019), which provides the frequencies of occurrence of 14,800 orthographic forms occuring in written material intended for Quebec elementary school children (28 textbooks, 24 booklets, 90 excerpts from children's literature), as well as a lexical spelling acquisition scale. The statistical estimates of the orthographic and grapho-phonological characteristics of words presented in Eqol_infra are similar to those in Manulex_infra: frequency and consistency of orthography-phonology associations (grapheme-phoneme, phoneme-phoneme, phonological rime), orthographic, phonological, graphemic and syllabic length of words, graphemic complexity, and orthographic neighborhood of words (Levenshtein distance). Lexical entries corresponding to proper nouns, onomatopoeias, and interjections were excluded from the analyses. With the exception of the following points, the coding and analysis principles are identical to those adopted for Manulex_infra (detailed on the pages devoted to this latter database):

• The phonemic distinction between /a/ and /ɑ/ is reintroduced
• For several words, the phonological codes are modified to reflect the distinction between /a/ vs. /ɑ/ and /o/ vs. /ɔ/ in Quebec French
• Schwa at the end of words are optional or silent
