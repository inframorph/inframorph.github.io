Major changes in Manulex_Infra version 2

• Separate G-Ph and Ph-G segmentations. In version 1 of Manulex_Infra, the description of the grapho-phonological associations was mainly thought in the reading direction, from grapheme to phoneme. The same associations were then used to analyze the associations between phonemes and graphemes (writing direction). However, this procedure causes problems especially -though not exclusively- when the words include silent letters. It should be remembered that the notion of consistency refers, in the case of Ph-G associations, to the probability of producing a particular grapheme from a given phoneme. When the phoneme is said to be 'silent' (silent letter), the speech signal cannot lead to the production of a silent letter and the descriptions of the associations must therefore be distinguished according to whether we are considering reading (G-Ph) or spelling (Ph-G). This is done in version 2 (see tab 'Understanding Manulex_Infra).

• Version 2 includes additional analyses on the consistency and the frequency of grapho-phonological associations on the final rime unit of words

• Additional analyses restricted to lexical forms corresponding to lexemes (lemmas), thus excluding inflected forms (see tab 'Understanding Manulex_Infra')

• For each word, the least consistent G-Ph or Ph-G association, and of the least frequent association are mentioned. Note that the least consistent association is not necessarily the least frequent association, and vice-versa.

• Modifications/corrections of several phonological codes, graphemic and phonological segmentations

• Removal of the distinction between the two 'a's (/a/ of 'patte' and /ɑ/ of 'pâte') when computing G-Ph consistency

• Words including the grapheme 'ai' (e. g., 'maison', 'lainage') can frequently be pronounced with the phoneme /E/ or the phoneme /e/. The consistency of the G-Ph association no longer takes this distinction into account.

• Introduction of the distinctions between the 'e' grapheme when obligatorily pronounced (compulsory schwa), always silent, or corresponding to an optional schwa (see 'phonetic codes' tab)

• Modifications of the syllabic segmentation of words in accordance with the coding of the ‘e’ as silent/optional or corresponding to a compulsory schwa

• The G-Ph consistency for 'e' with optional schwa ('reste', 'perle') is set to 100 since the 'e' may or may not be pronounced.

• In the case of Ph-G associations, the silent letters in internal position (e.g. 'm' in 'autumn', 'p' in 'baptism') are not predictable from the speech signal, and the Ph-G consistency is therefore set to zero

• Case of 'e' followed by two identical consonants. In version 1 of Manulex_infra, the orthographic sequences 'emm' and 'enn' were coded as graphemic groups whereas the 'e' followed by other doublets ('err', 'ett') were coded separately from the consonants that follow (for example 'e.rr' in 'terre'). This particular coding of 'emm' and 'enn' satisfied the segmentation principle aimed at highlighting inconsistencies in word pronunciation, as these two orthographic sequences were pronounced differently in 'antenne' and 'flemme' than in 'solennel' and 'patiemment'. However, given the high number of adverbs in 'emment' sharing the 'emm'-/am/ association (patiemment, évidemment, récemment), a word like 'femme' was coded as consistent. In version 2 of the database, the coding is standardized by marking the 'e' when followed by two identical consonants. For exemple, the word 'femme' is now coded as 'f.e[CC].mm.e' (with CC to indicate 2 identical consonants). Its low consistency results from the fact that 'e' followed by a doublet is usually pronounced /e/ or /E/. This coding is only realized for G-Ph associations, not for Ph-G associations, since in French, the presence of a doublet in not cued in the speech signal.

• Case of words including -eill or -eil not preceded by 'u' (e. g., 'abeille', 'bienveillant', 'sommeil'). The coding has been modified in 'eil' or 'eill'. In these groups the sequence 'ill' is always associated with the semi-vowel /j/, never with the consonant /l/

• The finals of verbs in -ent are, in the reading direction (G-Ph), segmented 'en.t' in the version 2 of Manulex-infra. This segmentation is comparable to that of the forms in '-ant', '-ont', '-ons', ..., (an .t, on.t, on.s)

• In order to eliminate some rare G-Ph or Ph-G associations, proper names are excluded from the analyses.
